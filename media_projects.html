FLIMP 160 - Film and Media in a Digital Age

This course serves as a introduction to the technology and aesthetics of film, video and digital audio proudction, providing the groundwork of pratical fundamentals of media production and post-production. The lecture section teches us topics and technqiues that would be further explored in the lab section of the course, culminating in 3 major projects where the students put those skills to the test, creating 3 different production assignments ranging in practicing certain production skills, such as audio recording, sound design, image composition and framing, and post-preduction editing, etc.

Project 1: Define a Place

The first major assignment of the course creating a video composed of 10-20 shots in total, with the goal for students being able to "define a space or location" through visually controlled and expressive shooting. This can be done through vawrious ways, such as conveying an emotional tone that the viewer's can feel in the location, claustrophobia, fear, energy, etc., or what intrigues the filmamker about a certain place and what happens in the location, or the physical appearance of the filming location, etc.

Project 2 - Audio Portrait

This assignment, students are to make a short audio portrait based on an interview that the we would record with a fellow classmates. primarily, we would do a pre-interview with said classmate, in order to define a theme for our audio piece. We'll try to identify something unique about their story that "grabs" us during the interview phase and enhance it in post-production editing phase. After the pre-interview, we will write questions to be prepared for the interview with the classmate and guide teh interview to exploring the theme we want our piece to be about in connection to the interviewee.
